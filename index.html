🚀 Getting Started
01
Export from Peloton
Log in at members.onepeloton.com, go to Profile → Workouts, then click Download Workouts. The .csv file downloads directly to your device. The file includes your complete workout history.
02
Import Your File
On the import screen, click the drop zone or drag your CSV onto it. The dashboard validates the file, then displays a status — green = ready. Hit Import Workouts to load.
03
Data Persists Automatically
Your workouts are saved to browser local storage. Refreshing the page restores your data instantly — no re-import needed. Use Import New in the header to load a fresh export anytime.
📊 Dashboard Tabs
Overview
Your all-time summary. Shows total workouts, hours, calories, cycling miles, best streak, and average watts. Includes a discipline donut chart, monthly volume bar chart, and a 52-week activity heatmap showing workout density by day.
Cycling
Cycling-specific analytics. Filter by instructor, ride type, duration, and year. Key metrics include Avg Watts, Watts/Min, Output, Output/Min, total miles, and heart rate. Charts show your watt trend over time, output distribution, top instructors by watts, best rides, and cadence by ride length.
💡 Watts/Min and Output/Min are the fairest way to compare rides of different lengths — a 20-min ride competes equally with a 45-min ride on these metrics.
Activity
Cross-discipline view. Total calories, walk count and mileage, strength session count, and average session duration. Monthly calorie chart, duration breakdown by bucket, and your top-10 longest consecutive-day streaks.
Workout Log
Full searchable, sortable history of every workout. Filter by keyword, discipline, or year. Click any column header to sort. Navigate pages with Prev / Next.
💡 Use the CMP checkbox column to select 2–3 rides, then click Compare → to jump straight to a side-by-side analysis.
Compare
Head-to-head comparison of 2–3 selected rides. Shows a Performance Radar (spider chart) normalizing 6 metrics against your personal bests, a metric table with winner highlighted green per row, and a verdict card showing which ride won the most categories.

⚡ Tips & Gotchas
Scenic & Stretching rides show 0 watts
Peloton doesn't record power for scenic rides, stretches, or meditations. These are filtered out of cycling analytics automatically but still appear in the log and contribute to calorie/time totals.
Heart rate data is partial
HR is only recorded if you wore a heart rate monitor. The Cycling tab's HR KPI shows how many rides had HR data — if it's low, HR comparisons won't be representative.
Comparing rides of different lengths
Always use Watts/Min and Output/Min as the primary comparison metrics. Raw output always favors longer rides — a 45-min easy ride will outscore a 20-min max effort.
Data stays in your browser only
Nothing is sent to any server. All data is stored in your browser's local storage. Clearing browser data or using a different browser will require re-importing. Incognito mode does not persist data.
Updating with new workouts
Request a fresh export from Peloton and use Import New in the header. The new file replaces the old data completely — Peloton's export always includes your full history.
Charts look wrong on first load
If charts appear empty or squashed, try clicking to a different tab and back — this triggers a redraw with correct dimensions. This is a known browser layout timing edge case.
